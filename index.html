Welcome!

I’m a graduate student in philosophy at the University of Arizona. I work primarily on issues at the intersection of feminist political philosophy, social metaphysics, and philosophy of constitutional law.

My current research explores a theory of gender equality that aims to take both the metaphysics (the gender part) and the political philosophy (the equality part) seriously. Along the way, I defend a realist account of gender rooted in trans people’s meaning crafting practice, a social meaning account of discrimination that situates the legal issue of discrimination within a broader political struggle for equality, and an equality-based account of trans feminism that goes beyond authority and autonomy. I also have a growing interest in the history of second-wave feminism.
