Welcome!

I’m a PhD student in philosophy at the University of Arizona. I work primarily on issues at the intersection of feminist political philosophy, social metaphysics, and philosophy of constitutional law.

My current research explores an account of gender equality that aims to take both the gender part (the metaphysics) and the equality part (the political philosophy) seriously, with a view to its implications for transgender equality in particular. Along the way, I defend a realist account of gender which grounds facts about a person’s gender identification in facts about their gender, not the other way around; a social meaning account of discrimination which situates the legal issue of discrimination within a broader political struggle for equality; and an equality-based account of trans feminism that goes beyond authority and autonomy. I also have a growing historical interest in the participation of—and controversies over—lesbian and trans women in the early days of the women’s liberation movement.
