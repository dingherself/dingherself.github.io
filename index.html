Welcome!

I’m a PhD student in philosophy at the University of Arizona. I work primarily on issues at the intersection of feminist political philosophy, social metaphysics, and philosophy of constitutional law.

My current research examines the unexplored ways in which transgender equality at once poses pressing challnges to, and sheds novel light on, the meaning of gender equality. Informed by the failure of an emerging body of case law in the U.S. to adequately conceptualize transgender discrimination, I develop a trans feminist account of gender equality that takes both the gender (the metaphysics) and the equality (the political philosophy) of gender equality seriously. Along the way, I defend a social meaning account of discrimination which situates the legal issue of discrimination within a broader political struggle for equality, as well as a realist account of gender that grounds facts about a person’s gender identification in facts about their gender, not the other way around. I also have a growing historical interest in the participation of—and controversies over—lesbian and trans women in the early days of the women’s liberation movement.
